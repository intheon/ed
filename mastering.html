Mastering

Mastering brings a lot of joy into my life. It has been described as a ‘dark art’ of audio and is the most commonly misunderstood process. Mastering is the final stage before tracks are submitted away from the studio realm and into the hands of the next processes, it is the last alteration to the sound of a record before the music and audio is considered ‘complete’ and basically ensures the music is ‘competitively loud’ and tonally balanced. As from 2013 I no longer master my own recordings or mixes.

The perspective an engineer can have on a record when being hired to objectively decide the best outcome for a mix, already prepared, is just so great. To hone in on the strengths and reduce the weaknesses and to bring out the best in someone else’s already beautiful work… There is no job satisfaction quite like this for me.

Mastering accounts for a large percentage of my work, I am confident in getting the best out of your audio.
